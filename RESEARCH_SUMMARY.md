### 2. Scientific Accuracy vs. Artistic Interpretation

**Why:**
- Scientifically accurate quantum mechanics is visually boring (probability clouds)
- Users expect recognizable metaphors (orbiting electrons, even if wrong)
- Educational value can be achieved through tooltips/content, not literal visualization
- Award-winning scientific visualizations prioritize engagement over accuracy

**Recommendation**: 70% artistic, 30% scientific accuracy

**Examples:**
- MIT's "Visualizing the Proton" uses artistic interpretation
- NASA visualizations compress scales for comprehension
- Powers of Ten takes massive liberties with scale and detail

### 9. Performance Optimization Techniques

**Essential Techniques Identified:**

1. **GPU Instancing** - Render thousands of identical objects efficiently
2. **Frustum Culling** - Don't render off-screen objects
3. **LOD (Level of Detail)** - Reduce complexity at distance
4. **Texture Atlases** - Combine textures to reduce draw calls
5. **Adaptive Quality** - Detect device and adjust settings
6. **Lazy Loading** - Load scenes as needed, not all at once

**Critical Finding**: Without these optimizations, mobile will fail
